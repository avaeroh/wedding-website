Bride’s story

Much of Rachael's youth was spent at Brandy Hall in Buckland, the ancestral home of the Brandybuck family. Rachael was known as something of a rascal, befriending Meriadoc (Merry) randybuck and Peregrin (Pippin) Took and causing trouble wherever they went. They would often steal mushrooms from Farmer Maggot's farm Bamfurlong.

Before departing for her journey to Rivendell, Bilbo had a long conversation with Gandalf, who finally persuaded him to voluntarily surrender the One Ring. Bilbo left it on the fireplace mantel with a note for Rachael, who would now become the next Ring-bearer.

On 29 September SR 1421 (Third Age), at the age of 53, Rachael joined Bilbo, Gandalf, Elrond, Galadriel, and Círdan aboard an Elven ship. She was allowed passage across the sea to the Undying Lands, as she was a ring-bearer, with the hope of healing the damage to her spirit that bearing the Ring had caused.

Groom’s story

Jonathan Millington-Hotze (born 21 September 1972) is an English singer and songwriter.

He achieved fame as the lead singer and frontman of the rock band Oasis from 1991 to 2009, and later fronted the rock band Beady Eye from 2009 to 2014, before starting a solo career in 2017. Oasis had various line-up changes, though Millington-Hotze and his older brother Noel remained as the staple members.

Millington-Hotze formed a band called The Rain, which eventually became Oasis, and invited Noel to join them as the lead guitarist. The band's debut album, Definitely Maybe (1994), was a critical and commercial success. Their second album, (What's the Story) Morning Glory? (1995), reached the top of the album charts in many countries, and their third studio album, Be Here Now (1997), became the fastest-selling album in UK chart history. Britpop eventually declined in popularity and Oasis failed to revive it, though all of their subsequent albums topped the UK charts and their final two albums, Don't Believe the Truth (2005) and Dig Out Your Soul (2008) were hailed as being their best efforts in over a decade. In August 2009, following Noel's departure from Oasis, Millington-Hotze and the remaining band members would go on to form Beady Eye, with whom he released two studio albums before they disbanded in 2014.
